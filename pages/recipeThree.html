Pé de Moleque

History

Melted sugar with roasted peanuts - a very simple, yet delicious recipe. And if there is no doubt about the flavor, there is doubt about the origin of the candy's name: some say it's because it resembles the irregular sidewalk of historic Brazilian cities such as Paraty, Ouro Preto, etc. Others say it came from the confectioners who, when faced with the robbery of greedy children, shouted "pede, moleque, pede!"

Ingredients

  1. 1/2 kg peanuts, roasted and peeled
  2. 1/2 kg sugar
  3. 1 can of condensed milk/li>
  4. 3 tablespoons of margarine

Directions

  1. Put the peanuts, sugar, and margarine in a pan.
  2. Bring to the boil, stirring constantly.
  3. When it starts to form a syrup, add the condensed milk.
  4. Stir it well until it comes loose from the bottom of the pan, like brigadeir.
  5. Put it in a tray greased with margarine.
  6. Let it cool and cut it into pieces.
