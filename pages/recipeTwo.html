Brigadier

About

Let's agree that brigadeiro is unanimous when the subject is sweet, because there is nothing more Brazilian than a good brigadeiro. Some places in Brazil call the candy negrinho, but the name brigadeiro wins by far. And the history of this candy is very peculiar. The chocolate balls were first made in Rio de Janeiro, for parties that raised money for Brigadeiro Eduardo Gomes' candidacy for the presidency in 1946. Although he lost the elections to Eurico Gaspar Dutra, he won a sweet homage

Ingredients

  1. 1 tablespoon butter
  2. 14 oz sweetened condensed milk (395 g)
  3. Â¼ cup cocoa powder (30 g)
  4. 1 cup chocolate sprinkle (160 g), as needed

Directions

  1. In a pot over low heat, melt the butter, condensed milk, and cocoa powder, stirring continuously until you can see the bottom of the pot for 2-3 seconds when dragging a spatula through.
  2. Pour onto a greased plate, then chill for 1 hour.
  3. Shape and roll the chilled mixture into balls
  4. Roll the balls in chocolate sprinkles.
  5. Enjoy!
